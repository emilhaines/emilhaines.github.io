About me

Hello! I'm a machine learning engineer curerently working at Flawless. Previously I completed a master's degree in physics at the University of Bristol, before pursuing a PhD in particle physics at UCL working within the ATLAS collaboration at CERN.

Research

The ATLAS experiment is a particle detector located on the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) at CERN, the world's largest and most powerful particle accelerator. During my PhD, my research focussed on developing novel techniques to explore and constrain new, or beyond the Standard Model, physics, using data from LHC collisions. More specifically, I was involved in a search for long-lived particles produced via exotic decays of the Higgs boson, that decay within the ATLAS inner detector. Outside of this analysis, I developed software to validate and study the performance of the ATLAS trigger system in identifying displaced signatures within the inner detector, and worked on the application of graph neural networks for the identification of jets produced by long-lived particle decays. I completed my studies in 2024.
