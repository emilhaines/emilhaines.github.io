About me

Hello! I'm a high energy physics PhD researcher at UCL working within the ATLAS collaboration at CERN. Previously I have completed a master's degree in physics at the University of Bristol. I am interested in combining machine learning with principles from physical theories to solve real-world problems.

Research

The ATLAS experiment is a particle detector located on the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) at CERN, the world's largest and most powerful particle accelerator. My research focuses on developing novel techniques to explore and constrain new, or beyond the Standard Model, physics, using data from LHC collisions. More specifically, I am involved in a search for long-lived particles produced via exotic decays of the Higgs boson, that decay within the ATLAS inner detector. Outside of my analysis, I have developed software to validate and study the performance of the ATLAS trigger system in identifying displaced signatures within the inner detector, and am currently working on the application of Graph Neural Networks for the identification of jets produced by long-lived particle decays. I am due to complete my studies in 2024.
