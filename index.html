About me

Hello! I'm a high energy physics PhD researcher at UCL working within the ATLAS collaboration at CERN. Previously I have completed a master's degree in physics at the University of Bristol.

Research

The ATLAS experiment is a particle detector located on the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) at CERN, the world's largest and most powerful particle accelerator. My research focuses on the development of novel selection algorithms to search for evidence of new, beyond the Standard Model, physics, using data from LHC collisions. More specifically, I am involved in a search for long-lived particles produced via exotic decays of the Higgs boson, that decay within the ATLAS inner detector. Outside of my analysis, I have developed software to validate and study the performance of the ATLAS trigger system in identifying displaced signatures within the inner detector. I am due to complete my studies in 2024.
